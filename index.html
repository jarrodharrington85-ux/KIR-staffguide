New to the registry?

This page is for staff who are new to the role. Read it before using any other part of this guide — everything else will make more sense once you understand the basics.

i
After reading this page, go to Filing flows overview to see how each type of filing moves through the system, then Data entry to begin your first filing.

What the registry is

The Kiribati Online Business Registry is the official list of all companies and business names registered in Kiribati. It is operated by the Ministry of Tourism, Commerce, Industry and Cooperatives (MTCIC) and maintained through the business.gov.ki website.

When a business registers, their details are recorded on the register and made available to the public. Keeping this information accurate and up to date is a legal requirement — and your job is to make sure that happens correctly.

What types of entities are on the register

Entity typeWhat it isForms used
Local companyA company incorporated in KiribatiCO- forms
Overseas companyA foreign company registered to operate in KiribatiCO- forms
Business nameA sole trader or partnership operating under a registered nameBN- forms

What your role involves

Your work falls into three main categories:

Daily

Processing filings

Clients submit forms through their online account. You check the information and enter it into the register.

Regular

Supporting clients

Approving new accounts, managing authority requests, topping up balances, responding to support issues.

Monthly

Compliance

Sending annual return reminders and breach notices to entities that have not filed on time.

Important things to know from day one

!
Never enter information that is not on a submitted form. If information looks wrong or incomplete, send it back to the client for correction.
  • The register is a legal record. Accuracy matters. If you are unsure about anything, stop and ask.
  • Some decisions must go to your supervisor or the Registrar. This guide will tell you when. When in doubt, ask before acting.
  • State-Owned Enterprises are different. If any request involves a State-Owned Enterprise, inform the Registrar before taking any action.

Technical issues

There are two types of technical issues you may encounter. The type determines who you contact and how.

Type 1 — Client support issues

i
Client support issues are questions or problems submitted by clients through the registry website. You answer these yourself using the Support Issues function. See Responding to client inquiries for the full procedure, and Support issue responses for guidance on how to respond to common questions.

Handle these yourself when a client:

  • Cannot find a form or feature on the website
  • Has a question about how to register or file
  • Has forgotten their username or password
  • Needs to know the status of their filing
  • Has a question about fees or payment
  • Needs help uploading documents

Type 2 — Platform technical issues

!
For platform technical issues, notify your supervisor first, then contact Paradigm Apps. Do not attempt to fix system or configuration issues yourself.

Escalate to your supervisor and then Paradigm Apps when:

  • The registry website is not loading or is showing an error
  • A form is not working correctly or is missing fields
  • A report is producing incorrect results or cannot be generated
  • You need a custom report that does not exist in the system
  • A system feature is not behaving as expected
  • Data appears to have been lost or corrupted

How to contact Paradigm Apps

When logging an issue with Paradigm Apps

Provide as much detail as possible so the issue can be resolved quickly. Include:

  • What you were trying to do when the issue occurred
  • What you expected to happen
  • What actually happened (including any error messages — copy the exact wording)
  • The date and time the issue occurred
  • Your name and the registry (Kiribati MTCIC)
  • A screenshot if possible

Compliance

Annual return cycle

i

The annual return compliance cycle runs every month. You will receive a Google Calendar reminder at the start of each month telling you what to do. All emails are sent from the shared registry inbox.

Every registered entity has an allocated return month — the month in which they must submit their annual return each year. Each month, you run two system reports which will tell you which entities:

  • are due to submit their annual returns (entering the cycle)
  • are overdue and need to submit their annual return with a late fee (in the cycle)
  • are in full breach and need to be removed (removal process)

You use these reports to send notification emails. The system determines who gets which notice based on where the entity is in the annual return compliance cycle.

How the compliance cycle works

The diagram below shows what happens to an entity from the time their return is due. An entity can exit the cycle at any point by filing their annual return.

!
Entities that do not file must be removed from the register. Providing extensions or failing to remove the entity is not legally allowed.

File management — before you start each month

The Compliance Tracker is a Google Sheet that records which entities have received notices. You must create a fresh copy each month. Never edit the template directly.

!
Rule: the template is never edited. Every month you make a copy of it, rename it, and work in the copy.

Start of month — what to do

i

At the start of every month you will receive a Google Calendar reminder.

Your tasks are the same every month: run two reports, then send emails.

The email tool determines which notice each entity receives based on how long they have been non-compliant.

1
Run the two reports

Log in to the registry. Go to ADMIN → REPORTS and download both of the following:

  • All Entities – AR Allocated Month — entities whose annual return is due this month.
  • All Entities – AR Non-Compliant — entities that have not yet filed and are overdue.

Save both files into this month's subfolder in Google Drive.

2
Open the email tool

Open the AR Compliance Google Sheet. You will see a custom menu called AR Compliance at the top of the screen.

Click AR Compliance → Import Reports from Drive.

The tool will read the two report files and prepare a list of entities for this month.

3
Prepare this month's emails

Click AR Compliance → Prepare This Month's Emails.

The tool will calculate which notice each entity should receive and populate the Review tab. Each row is colour-coded:

  • Green — pre-AR reminder (return due this month)
  • Yellow — Breach Notice 1 (one month overdue)
  • Orange — Breach Notice 2 (four months overdue)
  • Red — Breach Notice 3, final warning (six months overdue)
4
Review the list

Go to the Review tab and check the list. Each row has a Send? column set to YES.

Change YES to NO for any entity that should not receive an email this month (for example, if you know they have already filed but the system has not yet updated).

Do not change anything else in the list.

5
Send the emails

Click AR Compliance → Send Approved Emails.

The tool will send emails to all rows marked YES. A confirmation will appear when complete, showing how many emails were sent.

All emails are sent from the shared registry inbox. The sent folder is the record of what was sent and when.

6
Update the Compliance Tracker

Open this month's Compliance Tracker.

The tracker identifies where the entity is in the current month. The entity will remain in subsequent reports until it either files the outstanding annual return or is removed.

  • For entities that received Breach Notice 1 this month, add them to Tab 1 with their entity details and the date the notice was sent.
  • For entities that received Breach Notice 2 or 3, update their row with the date of the latest notice.
  • Remove any entities from the tracker that have since filed their annual return.
7
Update the correspondence record

For each entity that received an email, add an entry to the correspondence record in the registry system. Log it as an outward email. In the note field, record which notice was sent, for example:

"Breach Notice 1 sent for failure to file annual return."

See Correspondence record for instructions.

When to undertake the removal process

Each month, when you update the Compliance Tracker, check whether any entities have now reached the removal threshold — six months after Breach Notice 1 was sent.

!
How to identify entities for removal: in the Compliance Tracker, look at the date Breach Notice 1 was sent. If six or more months have passed and the entity has still not filed, they must be removed.

For the full removal process, refer to AR non-compliance removal.

i

Occasional procedure

Restoration

Service standard: 5 working days

Restoration allows a removed entity to be reinstated to the register. See Restoration eligibility for time limits and conditions before proceeding.

i
Persons who had authority over an entity will retain that authority after removal. Granting new authority is only needed if the request is being made by someone without existing authority.

Restoration after failure to file annual return

There are two processes depending on whether a waiver of the annual return requirement is requested.

Standard process (most common)

1
Review and grant authority (if needed)

Process any entity authority request per the Authority requests procedure.

2
Data entry — review the restoration filing

From the Document Entry tab, open the restoration filing (CO-16, CO-22, or BN-6).

If the client has requested a waiver of the annual return requirement, consult a supervisor. If the waiver is approved, use the exceptional process below instead.

Errors / docs missing
Click SEND BACK FOR REVISION
Conditions not met
Click REJECT (e.g. more than 2 years since removal)
No issues (business names with BN-3)
Update entity record with revised information and click SUBMIT
No issues (companies / business names without BN-3)
Click SUBMIT
3
Hold — do not send for data review yet

Inform the data reviewer not to process the filing until you have confirmed the client has submitted their late annual return. The client will receive an automatic email directing them to file within 14 days.

4
Check status of late annual return

Set a reminder. Once the return month passes, go to ADMIN → Reports and run the non-compliant report. Confirm the entity no longer appears.

5
Release for data review

Inform the data reviewer they can now proceed to review and approve the filing.

Exceptional process (companies only — waiver approved)

i
The exceptional process applies only to companies (not business names). Use only where it would be impractical or unfair for the client to submit an annual return — e.g. a creditor seeking to restore a company to reclaim a debt.
1
Steps 1–2 as per standard process above

Name compliance rules

Apply these rules when processing new registrations, re-registrations, and name change filings. The system performs basic automatic checks, but you must also review manually.

Companies and overseas companies (s.11, Companies Act 2021)

A company or overseas company name must:

  • End with the word "Limited" or "Ltd"
  • Not contravene any enactment
  • Not be identical or almost identical to the name of another company
  • Not be offensive or likely to mislead or deceive (in the opinion of the Registrar)
!
Additionally, a company must not use the name of another company that has been removed from the register in the last 2 years.

Business names (s.16, Business Names Act 2021)

A business name must not:

  • Suggest that the business enjoys the patronage of the Government, a local authority, or a foreign state
  • Be the same or almost the same as: a currently registered business name; the actual name of a body corporate (unless the body corporate is the applicant); a name for which another entity has filed an application that is yet to be determined
  • Be a prescribed name
  • Be the same or almost the same as a previously registered business name removed within the last 6 months — unless the former registrant consents

The Registrar must refuse to register if there are reasonable grounds to believe:

  • The applicant is unlikely to carry on a business under the name
  • Use of the name would contravene another written law
  • Use of the name would infringe another person's intellectual property rights (including trademarks)
  • The name is likely to mislead, deceive, or offend the public
i
If a name does not comply, consult a Registry Supervisor and/or the Registrar before proceeding. If denying the application, communicate the applicant's appeal rights under the Companies Act 2021 or Business Names Act 2021 as appropriate.
